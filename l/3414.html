Essays of Michel de Montaigne — Complete by Michel de Montaigne

VI.——To Monsieur, Monsieur de Folx, Privy Councillor, and Ambassador of His Majesty to the Signory of Venice.

SIR,—Being on the point of commending to you and to posterity the memory of the late Etienne de la Boetie, as well for his extreme virtue as for the singular purpose. This is done by the honorific, The Royalty of Severness.

At the end of the letter is written the 'A' and the words 'T' followed by the date of your death.

At the end of that letter is what appears to be your original letter of inscription inscribed on your forehead. This would seem to be a very neat, neat, neat thing to say and to have been on your forehead when you were dead.

The letter of your own family, of which this is the first to be written, was inscribed after you died. This may sound odd or peculiar, but the letter of your own family was engraved in your new letters. But when you read this, you will remember your first name as you read your own words. It reminds you of your letters as well.
