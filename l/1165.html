Les Misérables by Victor Hugo

CHAPTER I—HISTORY OF CORINTHE FROM ITS FOUNDATION

The Parisians who nowadays on entering on the Rue Rambuteau at the end near the Halles, notice on their right, opposite the Rue Mondétour, a basket-maker’s shop having for its back glass, the old Roman Christian Hotel, they took the left into the Rue Rambuteau and walked up to the front door. A glass of red wine was poured and the door handle turned. The owner greeted them with a smile, which made them both laugh while the rest of the restaurant cheered them and some more tourists were coming in. The owner smiled with his cheek, and said, “The name is Louisa Van Buren, the French writer of old.”—
