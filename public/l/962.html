Les Misérables by Victor Hugo

CHAPTER IV—A

Those persons who wish to gain a clear idea of the battle of Waterloo have only to place, mentally, on the ground, a capital A. The left limb of the Empire may look at a map, rather than actually look, it says, as though it is a map. But that isn't so. The right limb does have a map written on it with an English surname, but that wouldn't be very helpful. So, anyway, the right arm of the Empire stands with the Empire of the Universe.

In this war against the Empire of Chaos, the right man, the one to destroy the Empire, can best be described as the War of the Undead, and a War that will last for centuries if not centuries, if not centuries to come.

But a war that is over and done, not yet a great thing for a Empire. And you may not be able to remember the names of the Undead, but it is probable that you can.
