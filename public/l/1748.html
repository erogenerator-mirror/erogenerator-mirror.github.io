Notre-Dame de Paris by Victor Hugo

CHAPTER V. RESULT OF THE DANGERS.

Gringoire, thoroughly stunned by his fall, remained on the pavement in front of the Holy Virgin at the street corner. Little by little, he regained his senses; at first, for he was still walking straight. Then, with an effort, he raised his right hand. With the grace of an adolescent boy, he waved his index finger.

"Gringoire, please, I'm—"

"It's fine," the other youth interrupted him, "but hurry up because I don't feel well. You better come get with me," the other youth said to his friend in a more reassuring tone than he needed.

The youth started forward of his fellow walkers. He would have followed their lead but for a sudden movement and a sudden change of direction. The other youth stepped forward after him; they were both looking back again at the street where the holy Virgin was standing with a huge, empty smile on her face. Just as the other youth was about to head on her back and run away but now they were both looking at her, looking at an empty, dark hole in reality and an empty, dead place.

"Gringoire, please." The voice was filled with sorrow. "I'm going to die now," Gringoire cried as he reached the sidewalk towards the place where he would die.

"Gringoire, please," a familiar voice spoke back at him. "I'll be dying soon. I love you," the other youth cried back to him as he stood on the sidewalk.

He needed to be rid of them, just one of the young men who had gone too far with the spirit. They could not be the last ones that were to die. No, they were not going to pass up this opportunity once they reached the place where he would die. There was the chance that they would find it's own way back to hell.

"Please Gringoire, please. Don't. Let me die," Gringoire implored as he turned and ran as fast as his legs would carry him.

He saw a hand come over the street to steady him as he ran faster than he'd ever run before. He saw himself running to his final fate, where he would die—
