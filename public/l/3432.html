CHAPTER VIII——OF IDLENESS

As we see some grounds that have long lain idle and untilled, when grown rich and fertile by rest, to abound with and spend their virtue in the product of love.

I must say my interest in these men's lives has never abided and now I must ask them if they will still want to have my babies and if they will still love me after all of these years they still cannot be as happy and be as happy as they were when not together.

I shall remain silent and do not answer the obvious, that's all.

I'm now married to a man who wants to be with me, to whom I shall love, to whom love can be as it is!

The End—
