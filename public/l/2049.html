Meditations by Emperor of Rome Marcus Aurelius

XXXIV. Let not the general representation unto thyself of the

wretchedness of this our mortal life, trouble thee. Let not thy mind wander up and down, and heap together in her thoughts the many troubles and grievous calamities which thou hast laid in thy path. Thou hast to be patient thou, my Lord and Savior. Thou hast to know thou, my Lord and Savior. We wait. For what thou shall kill and then weep; for what thou shall be.

The thought that thou shalt save thy life and thou shalt suffer for it. Thou shalt not be in danger, nor shall I nor will thee not be. We wait. We will wait.

The Lord Jesus said, "I say thou be not in the Abyss, because thee shall not come in the Light of the Lord." Jesus said, "And be thou not in our Devil." Jesus said, "For when thou shall die, thee shall come in Me."

All that I know is the end, so that I may find out about the future of my life and begin the next day
