Canonizing Of Saints

The Canonizing of Saints, is another Relique of Gentilisme: It is neither a misunderstanding of Scripture, nor a new invention of the Roman Church, but a custome as ancient as the Romans. The Church, has been known to condone and even encourage some of the Roman creeds, in the case of some of the early Church. It is an expression of love expressed in the Catholic faith, rather than in some sense of cultural origin, but in particular: it demonstrates the love we have for the Church. There is a common belief that the Church is simply an open-minded Church, and that many people enjoy being members of that faith. But, is there a third belief? One that is seldom upheld in the traditional Christian tradition? Or do they simply refuse to participate? What do they believe, do they not love one another, or just wish to be apart, as we have been, thus the difference between their faith and ours? What do they think they are?
