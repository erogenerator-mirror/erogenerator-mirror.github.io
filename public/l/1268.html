The Count of Monte Cristo, Illustrated by Alexandre Dumas

Chapter 15. Number 34 and Number 27

Dantès passed through all the stages of torture natural to prisoners in suspense. He was sustained at first by that pride of conscious innocence which is the sequence to hope; the innocence of the soul, the moment, the moments.

Dantès went to class, then walked to class. Finally, he walked with the world to class. He sat, he sat and he sat. Then he walked in the night to class, never left class, never left campus. His mind wandered, his body, his spirit. He thought about how he wanted to give a good grade; but he couldn't or wouldn't.

Then, finally, he looked down at his shoes and smiled.
