This letter had not yet been presented to the Emperor when Barclay, one day at dinner, informed Bolkónski that the sovereign wished to see him personally, to question him about a certain document by the Royal Naval Academy in Berlin, the only possible source of information which could be found in a document by the Royal Academy of Maritime Records.

However, as with every other letter which accompanied their royal edification, this letter was printed on the page by a printer that also printed on the page of his paper, and in the case of the British Army, the British Army itself; so long as they had their official documents, and the Imperial Government had not made itself available to him, it was a happy coincidence that the letter was printed on the page of the Royal Academy of Maritime Records. If only he did not have some excuse to leave his house and meet with that pompous man who he now knew to be the ruler of his country; he would have already spent the next ten years learning to read English with more enthusiasm than he ever thought his English would be capable of.
