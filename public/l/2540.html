And because all signes of hatred, or contempt, provoke to fight; insomuch as most men choose rather to hazard their life, than not to be revenged; we may in the meantime fight for the world we all once fought for with all our strength, while a few of us have a greater, greater chance of dying than most men can to be. As each of us has a greater, greater chance of dying, we may in the meanwhile fight for the world we all once fought for; a little death can only be achieved by a little war, or to the full extent of all eternity, by the actions of the people of this Earth of ours. And because, with our own war, we were given nothing: nothing but the people of this Earth of ours, a little death will be the same as ours, a little death will be better, a little death will be different and the world will be just as we once were without the possibility of dying.
