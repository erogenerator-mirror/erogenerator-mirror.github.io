Grimms' Fairy Tales by Jacob Grimm and Wilhelm Grimm

THE FOX AND THE HORSE

A farmer had a horse that had been an excellent faithful servant to him: but he was now grown too old to work; so the farmer would give him nothing better than nothing. The idea of a great age of service for a friend was too great to pass up. With every bit of him that I owned, and as long as I could spend every minute of my life with him, I'd gladly live happily by the boy scout bookshelves.

His name is Michael, so who cared? Not. I loved him. He was a sweet, kind man; I would do anything for him. He was always so devoted to me. His parents would never hurt him. He even volunteered to go to a local youth group with him. Why? Why?

Of course, it wasn't too easy. We had to get dressed—and go grocery shopping. What was left of us was just too damn nice to waste.

My life in general and my life on the farm would never be the same.
