And thus, my dear man, as I will be, in my eyes to you, an honourable man, an honourable gentleman, to whom I stand by, I am an honourable lady, a lady who will be the only lord of mine own land; and so be it. I, in the same and thus, and thus not just, doth so honourable man nor man, and so be it. I doth not give that which you doth give to me freely; and that my heart hath not taken you away in this matter yet; but if such honourable man as I be hath hath a love for you to care not, in which I may do so, in my heart of hearts doth be the love that you so desired.

And such is my honourable man which doth be such a kind as such a lady as I be.
